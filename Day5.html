Day5 - Solo Projects

Day 5 was a major turning point in my coding journey — it was the day I finally took off the training wheels and created my first solo projects. Up until now, I had been following along with tutorials, completing exercises, and recreating websites with some guidance. But today, I took everything I had learned and put it into practice on my own, with no step-by-step instructions to follow. The results? My first personal projects: a CV website and the blog you're reading right now.

The First Solo Challenge: My Online CV

I decided to create an online CV for my first project. This was a simple and practical way to combine everything I had learned so far — HTML for structure, CSS for styling, and a bit of personal flair to make it stand out. It wasn't about being fancy; it was about applying the knowledge I had gained to build something useful.

The process was refreshing. I started from scratch with a blank file, just me, my code editor, and the ideas in my head. I made decisions about the layout, created the structure with headings, sections, and lists, and used CSS to make it look neat and presentable. There were definitely some hiccups along the way, but I felt a real sense of accomplishment as I saw it all come together

The best part? I now have a live, online CV that I can show off to potential employers or anyone who might want to know more about me. And it’s hosted on GitHub, which is another big win in itself. I took the leap from “just learning” to creating something real and usable — and I’m really proud of the result. You can check it out in my GitHub repository.

The Blog - A New Challenge

Alongside the CV, I decided to create this blog. Writing about my journey is not only a way to track my progress, but it’s also a project that allows me to practice my skills in a slightly different way. A blog has a different structure and style compared to a CV, and I was excited to build it from scratch, deciding how I wanted it to look and function.

This blog doesn’t look fancy or polished — it's simple and functional, and that's exactly how I wanted it. I created basic layouts, added some styling to make it more readable, and ensured it was structured correctly using HTML and CSS. Sure, I had to Google a few things along the way — there were a couple of syntax errors and little problems that popped up — but every time I ran into a roadblock, I felt more confident in figuring out how to solve it.

Learning on My Own: No Training Wheels

What made Day 5 so special wasn’t the fact that I completed two projects, but the fact that I did it on my own. For the first time, I wasn't relying on tutorials or structured lessons. I had no specific guide telling me what to do next. I just started coding, thinking about the design, experimenting with different approaches, and figuring out what worked. It was incredibly empowering.

Of course, I didn’t completely avoid external resources. When I ran into a problem I couldn’t solve, Google became my best friend. Whether it was a tricky CSS issue or a small mistake in my HTML, I turned to Google to help me troubleshoot. Searching for solutions helped me understand my errors, break down the problem, and implement the right fix. It was like having a little bit of extra help, but still working through the issues independently.

This experience really made me realise how much I’ve learned over the past few days. The ability to search for and solve problems is an essential part of being a developer, and it's something I feel much more confident about now.

The Buzz of Creating Something of My Own

One of the most exciting things about today was the feeling of accomplishment. As I was working on these projects, I couldn’t help but feel a sense of pride with each step. The more I built, the more I started to understand how all the pieces fit together. It wasn’t just about coding — it was about problem-solving, creativity, and persistence.

When I hit a roadblock, I didn’t get frustrated or give up. Instead, I saw it as an opportunity to learn, to push through and figure out how to make my project better. And when the final result came together, it felt like a huge win.

That’s the real magic of coding: the sense of creating something from scratch. I know my projects aren’t the most complex or advanced, but they’re mine. I took everything I’ve learned and applied it to something real, and that’s what makes it so rewarding.

Looking Ahead

As I look back at Day 5, I can’t help but feel excited for what’s to come. I’ve completed my first solo projects, but this is only the beginning. The skills I’ve gained so far are just the foundation, and now it’s time to build on them. Every project, no matter how simple, is another step forward in becoming a better developer.

The best part? I’m hooked. There’s something about the process of building, experimenting, and solving problems that is incredibly satisfying. Even though my projects are basic right now, I can feel myself getting better with each line of code. I’m learning, and I’m loving the journey.

Day 5 was a turning point for me. It wasn’t just about finishing a project — it was about taking ownership of my learning and building something I can be proud of. I can’t wait to continue this journey, learn more, and keep that buzz going.

Looking ahead to Day 6 and beyond, I’m ready to take on new challenges, experiment with more complex designs, and continue putting everything I’ve learned into practice. The excitement of creating something from scratch is what keeps me going, and I can’t wait to see what I’ll create next!
